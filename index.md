## About me

I am a doctoral student and research associate in the Department of English Linguistics at the University of Stuttgart. My doctoral dissertation is being advised by Judith Tonhauser.

My research interests lie in the areas of semantics, pragmatics and gesture studies, but I am also interested in psycholinguistics and sociolinguistics. I focus on phenomenas such as at-issueness, projection, iconicity and multimodal communication.

I study what kind of content iconic gestures contribute in multimodal communication and how these gestures interact with co-occurring speech. To do this, I conduct experiments in English within the project "Iconic of co-speech gestures in the taxonomy of not-at-issue content". This project is part of the DFG-funded Priority Program Visual Communication (SPP 2392 ViCom).

I hold a master’s degree from the Eberhard Karls University of Tübingen and a bachelor’s degree from the University of Stuttgart.
<br> For my master’s thesis, supervised by Britta Stolterfoht, I investigated the at-issue status of the content of iconic co-speech gestures expressing manner compared to the at-issueness of verbally expressed manner modification in German. To carry out this study, I also cooperated with Cornelia Ebert at the University of Frankfurt.
<br>My bachelor’s thesis, supervised by Ellen Brandner, concerns the distribution of the indefinite article with mass nouns in Swabian. It is related to the DFG-funded project "Partition and individuation in Germanic", to which I also contributed as a student assistant.
